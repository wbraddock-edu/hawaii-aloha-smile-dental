Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about dental care at Aloha Smile Dental.

Do you accept HMSA, UHA, and other Hawaii insurance plans? +
Yes! We accept most major insurance plans accepted in Hawaii including HMSA, UHA, Kaiser, AlohaCare Medicaid, Delta Dental, MetLife, Cigna, Aetna, United Concordia, Guardian, and Humana. We also offer a fee-for-service arrangement for patients without insurance and can discuss payment plan options through CareCredit. Call our office to verify your specific plan.
Are you accepting new patients? +
Yes, we warmly welcome new patients and their families. We make it a priority to accommodate new patients in a timely manner, including for new patient exams and emergency care. You can book your first appointment online or call (808) 555-0124.
Do you see children? How young? +
Absolutely — we love seeing children! We recommend the first dental visit by age one or within 6 months of the first tooth erupting. Early visits help us monitor development, provide preventive care like fluoride varnish, and most importantly establish a positive relationship with dental care before anxiety can develop. Dr. Kahale specializes in pediatric dentistry and our whole team is trained to make young patients feel welcome and safe.
What are your hours? Do you offer evening or weekend appointments? +
Our standard hours are Monday through Friday 8 AM to 5 PM. We offer extended hours until 6:30 PM on Tuesdays and Thursdays for patients who cannot come during the day. Saturday appointments are available from 8 AM to 1 PM by request. Emergency calls are answered 7 days a week.
What should I do if I have a dental emergency? +
For dental emergencies, call us at (808) 555-0124 immediately. For knocked-out teeth, store in milk or saline and come in within 30-60 minutes. For severe toothaches or swelling, call for a same-day appointment. We prioritize emergency patients and will see you the same day in virtually all cases. Do not go to the ER for dental pain unless it is severely affecting your breathing or swallowing.
How often should I get a dental cleaning? +
Most patients benefit from professional cleanings every 6 months. However, patients with gum disease, a history of frequent cavities, dry mouth, or certain systemic conditions may benefit from 3-4 month cleaning intervals. We'll recommend the right frequency for you based on your individual oral health status at your exam.
Is teeth whitening safe? +
Yes, when done professionally or with dentist-approved products, teeth whitening is safe. The main side effect is temporary sensitivity, which usually resolves within 48 hours. We screen patients for contraindications before whitening — certain types of discoloration don't respond well, and existing crowns or veneers won't whiten. Our consultation ensures you get the best results safely.
How do I manage dental anxiety? +
Dental anxiety is very common, and we're experienced in helping anxious patients. Please tell us about your concerns — we won't judge, and knowing helps us adjust our approach. We can explain each step before doing it, use nitrous oxide for relaxation, take breaks when needed, and use our gentle technique to minimize discomfort. Many patients who were anxious when they first came to us tell us they no longer dread dental visits.
Do you offer payment plans? +
Yes. We accept CareCredit, a healthcare credit card that allows you to pay over time with low or zero interest promotional periods. We also offer in-office payment arrangements for established patients. Our front desk team will work with you to find a payment approach that fits your budget — your dental health is too important to delay over financial concerns.
