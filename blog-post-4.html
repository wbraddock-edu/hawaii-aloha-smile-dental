What to Do in a Dental Emergency on Maui

By Aloha Smile Dental Team · April 29, 2024 · 6 min read

Dental emergencies are stressful under any circumstances, but being on vacation or recently relocated to Maui when a dental emergency strikes adds an additional layer of uncertainty. Knowing what to do in the first critical minutes and where to seek help can make a significant difference in the outcome of a dental emergency.

Knocked-Out Tooth (Avulsion)

A knocked-out adult tooth is one of the most time-sensitive dental emergencies. The tooth can potentially be reimplanted if you act quickly — ideally within 30-60 minutes. Handle the tooth only by the crown (the white part), never the root. Rinse it gently with milk or saline if dirty, but do not scrub it. Store it in milk, saline, or between your cheek and gum (not in plain water). Call your dentist immediately and get to the office as fast as possible. Time is critical. Note: knocked-out baby teeth in children are generally not reimplanted, but you should still call a dentist for guidance.

Toothache

A severe toothache is often a sign of either a cavity that has reached the nerve (pulpitis) or a dental abscess — a bacterial infection. Rinse with warm salt water. Over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen can manage pain temporarily. Do not place aspirin directly on the tooth or gum — this causes chemical burns. Call your dentist for a same-day emergency appointment. Do not ignore a severe toothache, especially if accompanied by swelling, fever, or lymph node tenderness, which indicate spreading infection requiring prompt treatment.

Broken or Chipped Tooth

Save any pieces if possible. Rinse your mouth with warm water. If there's bleeding, apply gentle pressure with clean gauze. If a sharp edge is cutting your tongue or cheek, dental wax (available at pharmacies) can temporarily smooth it. See your dentist as soon as possible — the urgency depends on the severity of the break. A minor chip without pain may not require same-day care; a break exposing the nerve causes significant pain and requires urgent treatment.

Lost Crown or Filling

A lost crown or filling is uncomfortable but not usually a crisis. The exposed tooth will often be sensitive to temperature and pressure. Dental wax or a temporary filling material (available at pharmacies) can protect the tooth temporarily. See your dentist within 1-2 days to have the crown re-cemented or filling replaced. Don't wait more than a week — the exposed tooth structure can shift, and the crown may no longer fit properly.
